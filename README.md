# SafeHer 🚺

SafeHer is a women safety and awareness application built as a submission for the Apple Swift Student Challenge 2026 using SwiftUI.

It started with a simple observation — almost every girl I know has some version of the same routine:
texting “reached home safe”, sharing live locations, clutching keys while walking alone, or pretending to be on a call in uncomfortable situations.

These aren’t habits. They’re survival instincts.

SafeHer was created to focus not only on emergency response, but also on preparation, awareness, and confidence through interactive learning and safety-focused experiences.

---

## 🌟 Core Idea

The goal of SafeHer is to make safety feel like a skill that can be practiced — not just a reaction during emergencies.

Instead of only relying on panic buttons or alerts, the app encourages users to:
- build situational awareness
- practice realistic scenarios
- develop safer habits
- stay calm under pressure

---

## ✨ Features

### 🎯 Practice Scenarios
Interactive real-world safety situations such as:
- walking alone at night
- cab rides
- crowded public spaces

Designed to encourage reflection and decision-making.

### ⏱️ Safety Quiz
Timed situational awareness challenges to test instincts and decision-making under pressure.

### 📅 Daily Challenge
Small daily safety tasks designed to build awareness habits consistently.

### 🧘 Calm Mode
Guided breathing exercises with responsive animations to help users stay grounded during stressful situations.

### 📋 Personal Safety Plan
Users can create a personalized emergency plan including:
- trusted contacts
- safe locations
- emergency action steps

All stored locally on the device.

### 💡 Safety Tips
Curated safety guidance across:
- commuting
- digital safety
- public spaces
- home safety

### 🏅 Progress Tracking
Track:
- streaks
- levels
- achievements
- learning progress

---

## 🔒 Privacy First

SafeHer is designed with privacy in mind.

- Runs offline
- No personal data collection
- No external data sharing
- Information stays on the user’s device
